PERMA:
The Daily Spine

Seligman introduced PERMA in his book Flourish ‚Äî not coincidentally, the name of this app. Each of the five pillars runs as a daily micro-exercise mapped to the climate resilience context. The PERMA-Profiler (Butler & Kern, 2016) is administered at Weeks 1, 6, and 12 as the primary outcome measure.

P

Positive Emotion

Climate application

Climate grief can narrow attention and foreclose positive feeling. Rebuilding the capacity for positive emotion ‚Äî joy, awe, hope, serenity ‚Äî is not denial of crisis. It is the psychological fuel for sustained action.

üåÖ Daily Exercise: Climate Awe Journal (3 min)Sunnie asks: "Find one moment of awe, beauty, or wonder connected to the living world today ‚Äî however small." Research shows awe counteracts the constricted attention of eco-anxiety and generates prosocial motivation.
E

Engagement

Climate application

Flow ‚Äî the state of total absorption ‚Äî is both a well-being builder and a reliable sign of intrinsic motivation. The ecological activities that create flow for each user become the daily practices that sustain engagement.

üåä Daily Exercise: Eco-Flow Finder (5 min)Sunnie asks: "What sustainability-related activity made time disappear this week?" Users identify and practice their eco-flow state ‚Äî their most intrinsically motivated form of climate engagement.
R

Relationships

Climate application

"Other people matter" (Peterson). Climate isolation is one of the most damaging dynamics for young people ‚Äî finding even one person who shares your concern changes the psychological landscape. The Climate Circles peer groups are the R anchor.

ü§ù Weekly Exercise: Climate Conversation Card (10 min)A Sunnie-generated conversation starter shared with one person in your life each week. Not to convince ‚Äî to connect. Calibrated from casual to close depending on intimacy level.
M

Meaning

Climate application

Meaning-focused coping (Ojala & Chen, Cambridge Ch.14) is the only coping style associated with BOTH better mental health AND greater climate engagement simultaneously. Purpose is the bridge between distress and leadership.

üß≠ Daily Exercise: Purpose Pulse (2 min)End-of-day Sunnie check-in: "Did today feel connected to something larger? Rate 1‚Äì10." Flags when meaning drops below 5 for 3 consecutive days and triggers a meaning-recovery practice.

Accomplishment

Climate application

Self-efficacy ‚Äî the belief that your actions matter ‚Äî is the strongest predictor of sustained pro-environmental behavior. Building accomplishment through small, visible wins creates the momentum for larger ones.

üèÜ Weekly Exercise: Eco-Achievement LogEvery Sunday, log one sustainability-related accomplishment ‚Äî however small. Displayed as a visual timeline. Regular acknowledgment of small wins builds the self-efficacy needed to attempt larger ones.

Connect. Adapt.
Innovate.

Julia Novy's CAN framework ‚Äî the core of Stanford's Change Leadership for Sustainability program ‚Äî identifies three orientations that define effective sustainability leaders. Purpose sits at the center of the triangle, anchoring all three. In this curriculum, CAN becomes a dedicated 3-week arc (Weeks 7‚Äì9) ‚Äî the pivot where inner healing becomes outer leadership.

CAN Framework ¬∑ Novy, Banerjee & Matson ¬∑ Stanford 2021

The triangle's geometry is intentional. Purpose ‚Äî your ecological values, your "why" ‚Äî sits at the center. Each orientation draws from it and feeds back into it. You cannot genuinely Connect without knowing your purpose. You cannot Adapt without purpose anchoring you through uncertainty. You cannot Innovate without purpose directing what you build toward. The New Leader develops all three orientations simultaneously, with purpose as the stable center.

Source: Novy, Banerjee & Matson (2021) used empathy interviews with 100+ sustainability leaders from business, government, and civil society to identify what New Leaders actually need. Stanford's SUST 220 course description states: "The course teaches students the Connect, Adapt and Innovate (CAN) orientations and other skills which enhance students' ability to cultivate resilience and well-being in their lives and to lead change in complex systems."

Academic Sources

Evidence Base

Every activity is traceable to peer-reviewed research. Primary sources anchor the architecture; supporting sources cross-validate specific practices.

Primary ¬∑ MHCCA / Simon Fraser University

CEDAR ‚Äî Climate-related Ecological Distress and Resilience Scale

The Climate Reactions Self-Assessment Tool developed by researchers at SFU and published by the Mental Health and Climate Change Alliance. 12 validated items across 4 dimensions: Affect & Emotional Response, Cognitive & Social Impairment, Social Support & Resource Access, Motivation & Behavioural Engagement. The only validated climate-specific distress AND resilience tool currently available. Dropout recommendations drawn directly from the MHCCA dimension guides.

Assessment engine for the entire curriculum. Personalization layer for Sunnie recommendations.
Primary ¬∑ Stanford University ¬∑ MDPI Sustainability 2021

Novy, Banerjee & Matson ‚Äî "A Core Curriculum for Sustainability Leadership"

Foundational paper for Stanford's Change Leadership for Sustainability Program. Introduces the New Leader four-ring model (Identity ‚Üí Perspective ‚Üí Capability ‚Üí Agency) and the CAN orientations (Connect, Adapt, Innovate) as the core of SUST 220. The paper's 100+ empathy interviews with global sustainability leaders validate the CAN framework against real-world practitioner need. Purpose as the center of the CAN triangle reflects the program's explicit normative goal of intergenerational well-being.

CAN triangle framework. New Leader identity model. All six module arc structure.
Primary ¬∑ Cambridge University Press 2024

Haase & Hudson (eds.) ‚Äî "Climate Change and Youth Mental Health: Multidisciplinary Perspectives"

Most comprehensive clinical text on youth climate distress. Ch.3‚Äì8: psychiatric, developmental, neurobiological, psychoanalytic, trauma, and CBT foundations. Ch.10: ACT, psychodynamic, and ecotherapy for climate distress. Ch.11: intersectional and ecological perspectives. Ch.14 (Ojala & Chen): meaning-focused coping as the dual-outcome coping strategy. Ch.15: social-ecological systems framework. Appendix B: Costs and Benefits of Activism Scale.

Clinical backbone for all modules. Distress spectrum. Meaning-focused coping evidence. Intergenerational framework.
Primary ¬∑ Positive Psychology ¬∑ Seligman 2011

Seligman, M.E.P. ‚Äî "Flourish: A Visionary New Understanding of Happiness and Well-being"

Introduces PERMA (Positive Emotion, Engagement, Relationships, Meaning, Accomplishment) as the framework for flourishing. Not coincidentally, the basis for this app's name. Each pillar mapped to a daily climate-specific micro-exercise. PERMA-Profiler (Butler & Kern, 2016) provides the primary outcome measure. The distinction between PERMA as a framework rather than a theory allows flexible, contextual application to climate resilience.

Daily micro-exercise spine. PERMA-Profiler as primary outcome measure for blind study.
Supporting ¬∑ ScienceDirect 2023

Lindqvist et al. ‚Äî Climate-Specific Internet CBT Randomized Controlled Trial

The only published RCT on internet-delivered CBT for climate distress. Effect sizes: depression d=0.87, stress d=0.76, quality of life d=0.79 over 8 weeks. ICBT participants also showed increased pro-environmental behavior post-treatment. Validates the digital delivery model and provides effect size benchmarks for power calculations in a future blind study. Critical finding: treating distress can increase climate engagement, not decrease it.

Delivery model validation. Blind study power calculation basis. Effect size benchmarks.
Supporting ¬∑ PMC 2021

Baudon & Jachens ‚Äî Scoping Review of Eco-Anxiety Interventions

Reviews 34 records. Five major intervention categories: resilience-building, action-encouraging, social connection, nature connection, grief-informed approaches. Creative expression, grief rituals, and group peer support are evidence-supported. The CEDAR Social dimension activities draw directly from the social connection and nature connection recommendations.

Cross-validates Modules I, III, IV, VI activities.
Supporting ¬∑ Taylor & Francis 2024

Terrien & Brouillard ‚Äî ACT for Climate-Induced Psychological Distress

Establishes ACT's six core processes as directly applicable to eco-anxiety. Specifically cultivates "psychological self-care that prevents climate fatigue or burnout" ‚Äî a critical function given the CAN arc's demands. ACT defusion (Module II), values clarification (Module I), and committed action (Module V and CAN Innovate week) all validated.

ACT-based activities across Modules I, II, V and the CAN arc.
Supporting ¬∑ PMC 2025

Qatar Youth Study ‚Äî PERMA Validation in Adolescents

N=502 high school students (ages 13‚Äì18). Validates PERMA-Profiler for youth populations. Finds anxiety and depression negatively associated with PERMA scores, confirming PERMA as a sensitive measure for detecting both distress and resilience in the target population of this curriculum.

Validates PERMA-Profiler use with youth. Blind study measurement justification.
