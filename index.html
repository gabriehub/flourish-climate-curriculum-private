Why PERMA + CAN together?

PERMA asks: what makes a person flourish? CAN asks: what makes a sustainability leader effective? The insight of v3 is that these are the same question at different scales. A person who is flourishing in PERMA terms ‚Äî feeling positive emotions, engaged, connected, purposeful, and accomplished ‚Äî is precisely the person who has the inner resources to Connect, Adapt, and Innovate in complex systems. PERMA builds the foundation; CAN is what you build on it.

Climate Awe Journal

Each morning Sunnie prompts: "Find one moment of awe, beauty, or wonder connected to the living world today ‚Äî however small." üî¨ Research Design ¬∑ Randomized Controlled Trial

Designing the
Blind Study

How would we rigorously test whether this curriculum actually works? A randomized double-blind controlled trial is the gold standard ‚Äî here is how we would design one for Flourish.ai's climate resilience module, including participant recruitment, randomization, blinding, measures, and ethical considerations.

Study Design Overview

A parallel-group randomized controlled trial (RCT) with two arms: intervention (full 12-week curriculum) and active control (general Flourish app use without climate module). Assessors blinded to group assignment. Users not told which arm they're in ‚Äî both receive a "personalized well-being journey."

  • N = 200 (100 per arm)
  • Duration: 12 weeks + 3-month follow-up
  • Design: Parallel RCT, assessor-blinded
  • Registration: ClinicalTrials.gov pre-registration

Participant Recruitment

Target: young people aged 16‚Äì28 who report moderate or higher climate concern. Recruited via Flourish app onboarding (climate concern screener), university partnerships, and climate-engaged social networks.

  • Inclusion: ages 16‚Äì28, smartphone access, moderate+ climate concern (score ‚â•4/10)
  • Exclusion: current psychotherapy for climate distress, active suicidal ideation
  • Stratified randomization by age (16‚Äì21 vs 22‚Äì28), gender, and baseline PERMA score
  • Diversity target: ‚â•40% BIPOC, ‚â•30% Global South

The Blinding Strategy

True double-blinding is impossible (users know if they're doing climate content). We use partial/assessor blinding ‚Äî the strongest feasible design for a behavioral intervention:

  • Users: told they're in one of two "personalized well-being programs" ‚Äî not told which is the climate module
  • Assessors: fully blinded ‚Äî outcome measures administered by researchers with no access to group assignment
  • Sunnie: identical interface in both arms ‚Äî control group Sunnie does not reference climate
  • Analysis: intention-to-treat (ITT), blind to group until analysis complete

Control Arm Design

The control arm receives Flourish's existing evidence-based content ‚Äî CBT, ACT, PERMA micro-exercises, mindfulness ‚Äî but without the climate frame. This tests whether the climate-specific components add value above and beyond general positive psychology. Both arms receive equal "dose" of app time.

  • Same daily micro-practice structure (5‚Äì8 min)
  • Same PERMA weekly check-ins
  • Same Sunnie interaction cadence
  • No CAN arc, no ecological identity work, no climate distress content

Ethical Considerations

Climate distress research with minors requires careful ethical scaffolding:

  • IRB: Full IRB review required; separate parental consent protocol for under-18s
  • Safety monitoring: Sunnie monitors for clinical-level distress in both arms; automatic escalation protocol if PHQ-9 or GAD-7 scores indicate clinical need
  • Debriefing: Post-study, control participants offered the full climate curriculum
  • Data: De-identified, GDPR/HIPAA compliant, 5-year retention maximum
  • Withdrawals: Right to withdraw without consequence at any time

Follow-Up Protocol

One of the weaknesses of existing eco-anxiety studies (including Lindqvist et al.) is short follow-up. This study includes a 3-month follow-up assessment to test maintenance of gains:

  • Week 12: endline (primary outcome)
  • Week 24 (Month 6): follow-up ‚Äî full PERMA + Climate Distress Thermometer
  • Pro-environmental behavior diary: 2-week diary at baseline, Week 12, and Week 24
  • Hypothesis: CAN arc's concrete action commitment (One Real Thing) will predict PERMA Accomplishment at Week 24 above and beyond Week 12 scores

1

Pre-register on ClinicalTrials.gov. IRB approval. Finalize randomization algorithm. Train Sunnie on both intervention and control protocols. Pilot test with 10 users per arm for UX and safety.

2

Open recruitment via Flourish app climate screener. Stratified randomization. Week 1 PERMA-Profiler, Climate Distress Thermometer, PHQ-9, GAD-7, Ecological Identity Scale administered. Allocation concealment maintained ‚Äî participants told they're starting a "personalized well-being journey."

3

12-week curriculum runs. Weekly PERMA check-ins collected. PHQ-9/GAD-7 safety monitoring at Weeks 4 and 8. Mid-point PERMA-Profiler at Week 6. Fidelity monitoring: Sunnie logs engagement rates, completion of each module activity, and departure from protocol.

Week 24 follow-up assessment. Control arm offered full climate curriculum (ethical obligation). Analysis of follow-up data. Pre-registered primary analysis published regardless of outcome. Secondary analyses (CAN self-efficacy, ecological identity, PEB diary) published separately.

üî¨ Key Hypotheses
H1 (Primary): Intervention arm will show significantly greater improvement in PERMA total score from Week 1 to Week 12 compared to control (expected d ‚â• 0.5).
H2: Climate Distress Thermometer scores will decrease significantly more in the intervention arm (expected d ‚â• 0.6, based on Lindqvist et al. effect sizes).
